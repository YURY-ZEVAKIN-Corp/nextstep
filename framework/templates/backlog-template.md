## Status Guidance

- `Draft`: newly captured
- `Ready`: meets Definition of Ready and is mature enough to become a requirement
- `In Progress`: being actively refined or executed
- `Blocked`: cannot proceed due to a dependency or issue
- `Closed`: intentionally not pursued or completed through the framework

## Definition Of Ready (DoR)

A backlog item can move to `Ready` only when all of the following are present:

- Clear description and business value
- Defined acceptance criteria
- Size estimate
- Identified dependencies
- Understood test approach
- No blocking open questions

## Type Guidance

- `Feature`
- `Bug`
- `Tech Debt`
- `Research`
- `Operational`

## Assignment Guidance

- Every backlog item must have exactly one named assignee.
- Assignees must be team members listed in the project or portfolio RACI/skill matrix.
- Team members may be assigned to backlog items in multiple projects.
- Every backlog item should be assigned to a sprint identifier (for example `SPRINT-2026-06-A`) or `Unscheduled`.
- If any Definition of Ready criterion is missing, keep status as `Draft` and continue refinement.
